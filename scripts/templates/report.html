Method & Definitions

Methodology

On-Time vs Promise
A shipment is "on time" if its actual delivery datetime is on or before the promised delivery datetime. The promise is calculated from the dispatch date plus the carrier's stated transit days, respecting the carrier's day-type definition (working days or calendar days).

Peak Window
{{ metadata.peak_window }}. This period captures Black Friday, Cyber Monday, and the pre-Christmas delivery surge — typically the highest-volume and highest-pressure period for UK parcel carriers.
Data Sufficiency
A minimum of {{ sufficiency_threshold }} shipments is required per slice for high-confidence reporting. Slices below this threshold are shown but flagged as "low confidence" and should be interpreted with caution.
Regional Granularity
Performance is measured at the UK postcode area level (the first 1-2 letters of the postcode, e.g., "SW", "BA"). This provides a meaningful geographic comparison without requiring full postcode-level data.
Data Source
Third-party shipment tracking data. All timestamps are in UTC.
Shipment Scope
Only shipments with status "Delivered" are included in KPI calculations. Failed, returned, and lost shipments are excluded from performance metrics. Total shipments analysed: {{ "{:,}".format(metadata.total_shipments_analysed) }}.
